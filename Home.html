WHY FRUITS AND VEGETABLES ?

“The sweetest fruits grow at the top of a tree so that only those who deserve them can reach them.”
 In the ruins of a prehistoric village near Jericho, in the West Bank, scientists have discovered remains of figs that appear to be the earliest known cultivated fruit crop. These figs, grown approximately 11,400 years ago, represent what may be the first evidence of domesticated food production at the dawn of agriculture.
discovery marks a significant milestone in human history as communities began transitioning from foraging to farming, laying the foundation for settled societies and the rise of civilizations. The cultivation of figs suggests early humans were starting to understand and manipulate their environment to produce food.
Further research indicates that other fruit crops, such as bananas and mangoes, were likely cultivated around 10,000 years ago.
These findings highlight the expanding agricultural knowledge of early communities and provide valuable insights into the origins of agriculture and the development of human societies.
